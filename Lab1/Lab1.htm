Interesting Articles

  1. Mashups: The new breed of Web app 
    2. This article covers the idea of mashing two different technologies, citing similarities to music and borrowing the term where two different songs will be mashed together. This article covers many different use cases for this concept, such as Mapping, video/photo, news, and shopping mashups.

This article explains that in mashups, multiple providers of content are involved, not just the core website itself. (Google maps being embedded in another site means that Google is now a content provider in that embedded site).

The article explains multiple technologies that help support mashups, and the technical challenges that are involved in intergrated multiple providers in one site. Such that the mashup site must ensure that the content is consistent and usable within. The article has an important discussion on how there must be clear emphasis on security, privacy and quality for the users.
  2. The Dirty Little Secrets of Search  
    3. When internet searching and indexing exploded, there was an unbelievable amount of people and companies trying their hardest to make their site appear as the number one search result on Google. This article details an occurance where general search results seem to always point to the "J.C. Penney" store website. Though they never openly admitted it, It was discovered that "J.C. Penney" had been exploiting the Google PageRank algorithm, by having links to their store scattered across the web in completely unrelated websites. Google strongly opposes this practice, and they will punish any site by restricting the rank of their site if they are caught performing this exploit.

I wrote a paper in IT279 covering the Google PageRank algorithm, how it worked, how people tried to game the algorithm unsuccessfully, and somehow missed this story of a large corporation successfully pulling it off. So instead of making it easy for people to shop online and becoming the best retailer, their hired web marketing agency cheated. Not a good look J.C. Penney.
